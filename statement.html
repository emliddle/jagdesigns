Artist Statement

On the work.

Jim Gregory

Glass & Steel Artist

Abiquiu, New Mexico
jagdesigns.com
jagdesigns68@gmail.com

View the Work → Biography → Commission Inquiry →

I begin with a clear vision. Before I touch any material, I know what I am trying to make: the form, the scale, the feeling it should carry in a space.

Then the material takes over.

Glass under heat does not do exactly what you tell it to. Steel forged by hand responds in ways that are consistent but never identical. I work the forge to draw out metal with qualities I cannot get any other way -- textures and forms that are made, not manufactured. I have spent years learning to read those changes, to recognize when the material is telling me something better than what I planned. The final design is always a collaboration between what I intended and what the material revealed along the way.

Nothing gets thrown away. The scrap from one piece becomes the starting point for the next. A cut end, an offcut, a fragment of glass: I look at what is left and see the beginning of a new vision. The work is a continuous cycle, each piece feeding what comes after it.

This is why no two pieces are alike, and why the process never really stops.

I engineer my public work to hold up to weather and decades. But I let the making determine what it ultimately becomes.

The final design is always a collaboration between what I intended and what the material revealed along the way.

Jim Gregory
